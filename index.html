My name is Paul Meinshausen

I am a data scientist who cares more about science than I do about data. But I don't think there's just one Science. Paul Feyeraband put it this way in his book Against Method:

"Successful research does not obey general standards; it relies now on one trick, now on another; the moves that advance it and the standards that define what counts as an advance are not always known to the movers. A theory of science that devises standards and structural elements for all scientific activities and authorizes them by reference to 'Reason' or 'Rationality' may impress outsiders -- but it is much too crude an instrument for the people on the spot, that is, for scientists facing some concrete research problem."

At a more practical level, I love building software that can approximate a human-devised solution to a problem and implement it at scale. Then I love taking the data that is generated by that software and exploring and exploiting it to improve the software and the solution to the original problem. Even more exciting is when I can use statistical analysis of data to generate solutions for problems that weren't recognized or considered when I started working.

I've practiced data science all over the world, and especially in Asia. If you want to know more about me, you can see a short bio here.
